About me

I entered oceanography via physics and applied mathematics. My typical regions of study are the Southern Ocean and (recently) the North Atlantic, which are both climate-relevant regions that feature strong exchanges of heat and carbon between the atmosphere and the deep ocean. My toolbox includes a hierarchy of approaches, from simple pen-and-paper representations to high-resolution adjoint models. I also use a few machine learning techniques (that is, algorithmic clustering) to better understand oceanic data. Outside of work, I spend time with my wife and young son, squeezing in some reading and guitar playing where possible. We live in a village just outside of Cambridge, UK.
